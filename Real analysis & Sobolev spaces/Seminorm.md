
# Definition: Seminorm

**Type:** #definition  

## Definition
- The seminorm of a [[Normed vector space|vector space]] $V$ is a function $$p:V\mapsto[0,\infty),$$ satisfying:
	- Positive [[Homogeneity]] $$p(\lambda u)=|\lambda|p(u), \ \forall \lambda \in \mathbb{R},u\in V$$
	- [[Triangle inequality]] $$p(u+v)\leq p(u)+p(v), \ \forall u,v \in V$$

- A seminorm may vanish on non-zero elements, i.e., the [[Norm]] may be zero, while the vector is not a zero vector
