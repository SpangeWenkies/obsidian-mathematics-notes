
# Theorem: Girsanov's theorem

**Type:** #theorem  

## Statement
- If a [[Measure]] $\mathbb{Q}$ is [[Absolute continuity|absolutely continuous]] w.r.t. measure $\mathbb{P}$ , then every $\mathbb{P}$-[[Semimartingale]] is a $\mathbb{Q}$-semimartingale

## Proof Idea (optional)


## Consequences / properties
- This can be used in [[Change of measure]] from $\mathbb{P}$ to $\mathbb{Q}$ 
	- The [[Radon-Nikodym theorem|R-N ]]derivative $\frac{d\mathbb{Q}}{d\mathbb{P}}$ can by Girsanov's theorem be used as the needed [[Likelihood ratio]] in the change of measure:$$E_{\mathbb{Q}}[f]=E_{\mathbb{P}}[f\frac{d\mathbb{Q}}{d\mathbb{P}}]$$
