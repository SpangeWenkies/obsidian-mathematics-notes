
# Definition: orthonormal basis

**Type:** #definition  

## Definition
- Take $S$ as a [[basis]] to a space equipped with an [[Inner product]], then it is orthonormal if: $$\text{for any }e_{1},e_{2}\in S, \ <e_{1},e_{2}> = 
\begin{cases}
      1 & e_{1}=e_{2}\\
      0 & \text{otherwise}
    \end{cases}   
$$
- common notation is $$<e_{1},e_{2}> = \delta_{ij}:=
\begin{cases}
      1 & i=j\\
      0 & i \neq j
    \end{cases}$$
	but be careful in using this $\delta_{ij}$ as these are not the elements of the [[Set identity|identity map]] $id_{\mathcal{H}}$ w.r.t. the basis $S$, but $\delta_{ij}$ is just bi-linear map and not the [[endomorphism]]
